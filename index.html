Staying on protocol

The Shred, on the road

Georgian and Armenian food is quietly one of the most protein-friendly cuisines for a cut. Here's how to hold the line without being the difficult one in the group.

🍢

Lean on the grill

Mtsvadi and Armenian khorovats are grilled, lean, huge. Default to these — easy 40–50g protein hits.

🧀

Cheese & yogurt

Matsoni yogurt and fresh sulguni at breakfast — high protein, low sugar. Skip the bread, keep the cheese.

🫘

Beans & walnuts

Lobio bean stew and walnut dishes (pkhali) carry protein + fibre — useful on the mountain guesthouse nights.

🥾

Training stays easy

Days 3–5 are active hikes (Ushguli, Koruldi, Chalaadi) — cardio handled. Tbilisi & Yerevan hotels have gyms or day-passes nearby.

🍷

The wine reality

The qvevri tasting is core to the trip — don't skip it. Budget it as treat days (Day 2, Day 6) and tighten around them.

📲

Log on Healthify

Khachapuri and lavash are the carb traps. Pre-log them so macros stay honest — enjoy it intentionally, not by accident.

What you'll eat

The table

Two great underrated food cultures — and unusually kind to vegetarians. The shortlist worth seeking out.

Khachapuri

The cheese-bread icon. Adjaruli is a boat of molten sulguni with an egg on top. A once-a-trip indulgence — share it.

Khinkali

Soup dumplings twisted at the top. Grab the knot, bite, sip the broth. The Day 1 welcome staple.

Mtsvadi / Khorovats

Georgian & Armenian grilled skewers — the protein backbone of your Shred plan. Order with tkemali.

Vegetarian

Pkhali

Beet, spinach or bean pâtés bound with walnuts & herbs, topped with pomegranate. Fully veg, beautiful.

Vegetarian

Lobio & Lobiani

Spiced bean stew or bean-stuffed bread. Hearty, protein-rich, vegetarian comfort food.

Vegetarian

Dolma & Lavash

Vine leaves with rice & herbs, fresh-baked lavash (made on Day 8). Plenty of meat-free Armenian plates.

🍷

The wine isn't a side note — it's the story. Georgia is the birthplace of wine, fermented in buried clay qvevri for 8,000 years. The Kakheti tasting (Day 2) is a signature — try the amber/orange wines even if you drink red.
