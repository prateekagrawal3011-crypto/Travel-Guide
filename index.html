Staying on protocol

The Shred, on the road

Good news: Georgian and Armenian food is quietly one of the most protein-friendly cuisines for a cut. Grilled meats, fresh cheese, yogurt, beans, walnuts, herbs. Here's how to hold the line without being the difficult one in the group.

🍢

Lean on the grill

Mtsvadi (Georgian skewered pork/veal) and Armenian khorovats are grilled, lean, huge portions. Default to these for dinner — easy 40–50g protein hits.

🧀

Cheese & yogurt are friends

Matsoni (Georgian yogurt) and fresh sulguni cheese are everywhere for breakfast. High protein, low sugar. Skip the bread basket, keep the cheese.

🫘

Beans & walnuts

Lobio (bean stew) and walnut-heavy dishes (pkhali, satsivi) carry protein + fibre. Useful on the mountain guesthouse nights when grill options thin out.

🥾

Training stays easy

Days 3–5 are active hikes (Ushguli, Koruldi ridge, Chalaadi glacier) — that's your cardio handled. Tbilisi & Yerevan hotels have gyms or there are day-pass options nearby.

🍷

The wine reality

The qvevri tasting and toasting culture is core to the trip — don't skip it entirely, that's the experience. Budget it as your treat days (Day 2, Day 6) and tighten around them.

📲

Log on Healthify

Khachapuri and lavash are the carb traps. Pre-log them so the day's macros stay honest — one cheese-bread can be a 600-cal hit. Enjoy it intentionally, not by accident.

What you'll eat

The table

Two of the great underrated food cultures — and unusually kind to vegetarians (handy for the family back home you're always feeding veg). Here's the shortlist worth seeking out.

Khachapuri

The cheese-bread icon. Adjaruli version is a boat of molten sulguni with an egg cracked on top. A once-a-trip indulgence — share it.

Khinkali

Soup dumplings, twisted at the top. Grab the knot, bite, sip the broth. The Day 1 welcome-dinner staple.

Mtsvadi / Khorovats

Georgian & Armenian grilled skewers — the protein backbone of your Shred plan. Order with tkemali plum sauce.

Vegetarian

Pkhali

Beetroot, spinach or bean pâtés bound with walnuts & herbs, topped with pomegranate. Beautiful and fully veg.

Vegetarian

Lobio & Lobiani

Spiced bean stew (lobio) or bean-stuffed bread (lobiani). Hearty, protein-rich, vegetarian comfort food.

Vegetarian

Armenian dolma & lavash

Vine leaves stuffed with rice & herbs, fresh-baked lavash (you'll see it made on Day 8). Plenty of meat-free Armenian plates.

🍷

The wine isn't a side note — it's the story. Georgia is the birthplace of wine, fermented in buried clay qvevri for 8,000 years. The Kakheti tasting (Day 2) is one of the trip's signatures. Amber/orange wines are the local specialty — try them even if you're a red drinker.
