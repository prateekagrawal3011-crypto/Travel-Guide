Shred protocol, on the road 食事

Japan is workable for high-protein-but-watch-carbs — it just takes intent. The konbini is your anchor; group meals and festival nights are "enjoy, don't stress."

🍱 The konbini playbook

7-Eleven, Lawson, FamilyMart — genuinely your best ally for both budget and macros.

  • Grab: salad chicken (sasami), boiled eggs, edamame, grilled fish onigiri, Greek-style yogurt
  • Use it for: breakfasts, travel-day meals, and topping up protein around indulgent group dinners
  • Backup: carry a protein bar or two for the long Kyushu bus days (the Takachiho leg especially)

Days 26–31 are self-run — no included meals, long bus mornings, and small-town dinners that close early.

Know before you go 準備

The practical spine — visa, money, knee strategy, heat, and the few things that genuinely matter on the locked itinerary.

🛂 Visa & entry ✓ GRANTED

Sorted via Atlys — a Temporary Visitor visa, single entry, 15-day stay, as a sticker in your passport. Nothing left to apply for; just carry the passport.

  • Carry the passport (visa sticker inside) — it's single-entry, so once you're in, don't leave and re-enter Japan mid-trip
  • 15-day stay = your exact 18 Jul→1 Aug window (arrival = day 1). It fits with no buffer — fly out on the 1st as planned
  • It's a sticker, not an eVISA — no online Issuance Notice to show at the airport
  • Register Visit Japan Web before flying for immigration/customs QR codes

💴 Money & connectivity

Japan is card-friendly but still part-cash, especially tiny bars and stalls.

  • IC card (Suica/Icoca) — tap for all metro/local trains; load ¥5,000–7,000
  • ATMs: 7-Eleven & Japan Post reliably take foreign cards
  • Tax-free on purchases over ¥5,000 (passport) — ~10% saving; do it at each store
  • eSIM — activate on landing at Haneda

🦵 Knee strategy

The itinerary is built knee-aware. Two days need a plan.

  • Fushimi-Inari (24th): stay at the base — the famous dense torii are there; skip the 2–3h summit
  • Kyushu leg: viewpoints and boardwalks (Aso crater rim, Takachiho gorge rim) — no forced hikes; the gorge rowboat is arms, not knees
  • No single high-risk day any more — but expect substantial walking and uneven surfaces throughout: Ninenzaka, Meiji’s gravel paths, Arashiyama, Fushimi-Inari’s base, Takachiho’s stairs and festival crowds all add up

🌡️ Heat & weather

Late July = hot and humid (28–34°C), tail of the rainy season into peak summer. No snow anywhere you go.

  • Light breathable clothes, compact umbrella, cooling towel, sun protection, hand fan
  • Kyoto (basin) is the sweatiest stretch; Aso's caldera (500–900m) takes the edge off, but Kyushu cities run as hot as Kansai — hydrate hard
  • Hydrate relentlessly, especially on festival and shrine days

🎆 The two festivals

You landed on both by luck — they fall on your Kyoto days.

  • Gion Matsuri (24th): the Ato parade runs in the morning, before the group reaches Kyoto — seeing the floats is unlikely unless the tour schedule changes; treat as a long-shot bonus
  • Tenjin Matsuri (25th): conditional on your Tru Leader confirming the day’s finish time — Kyoto→Osaka dash for the boats + ~3,000 fireworks. Nail the exit (linger, then walk to Umeda)

🏨 Where you sleep

The tour covers 6 nights (20–25). You arrange the rest.

  • 18–19 Jul: Tokyo — simplest via TruTravels at the joining hostel (GRIDS Ueno; email info@trutravels.com)
  • 26–31 Jul (self-booked): Kumamoto ×2 · Takachiho ×1 · Beppu ×1 · Fukuoka ×1 · Namba ×1 (the 31st — near the Nankai line for the 06:00 KIX run). Book all six now — late July is peak
  • Insurance: compulsory for TruTravels (min US$200k medical/evac) and strongly recommended for Japan — not a stated Japanese visa requirement unless the current Mumbai VFS checklist asks for it
