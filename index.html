Shred protocol, on the road 食事

Japan is workable for high-protein-but-watch-carbs — it just takes intent. The konbini is your anchor; group meals and festival nights are "enjoy, don't stress."

🍱 The konbini playbook

7-Eleven, Lawson, FamilyMart — genuinely your best ally for both budget and macros.

  • Grab: salad chicken (sasami), boiled eggs, edamame, grilled fish onigiri, Greek-style yogurt
  • Use it for: breakfasts, travel-day meals, and topping up protein around indulgent group dinners
  • Backup: carry a protein bar or two for Koyasan, where the food is all-vegetarian

Day 26's included dinner (and likely next breakfast) is shojin ryori — beautiful Buddhist vegetarian cuisine, but lower in protein than your usual.

  • Lean on tofu and koya-dofu for what protein there is
  • Carry backup bars from a city konbini
  • One day off-target won't derail anything — enjoy the cultural experience
06

Know before you go 準備

The practical spine — visa, money, knee strategy, heat, and the few things that genuinely matter on the locked itinerary.

🛂 Visa & entry

Indian applicants in Mumbai now submit through VFS Global by appointment (the consulate stopped direct/walk-in submissions in 2026); you're using Atlys (concierge).

  • Needs: passport (6mo), 45×45mm square photo (not Schengen size), detailed day-by-day itinerary (most scrutinised), flight + hotel bookings, bank statements, ITR, employer leave letter, cover letter
  • Realistic 3–6 weeks end-to-end in summer — apply now
  • If issued an eVISA: you must show the Visa Issuance Notice live online on your phone at the airport (internet required) — a PDF, screenshot or printout is not accepted (Mumbai Consulate guidance)
  • Register Visit Japan Web before flying for immigration/customs QR codes

💴 Money & connectivity

Japan is card-friendly but still part-cash, especially tiny bars and stalls.

  • IC card (Suica/Icoca) — tap for all metro/local trains; load ¥5,000–7,000
  • ATMs: 7-Eleven & Japan Post reliably take foreign cards
  • Tax-free on purchases over ¥5,000 (passport) — ~10% saving; do it at each store
  • eSIM — activate on landing at Haneda

🦵 Knee strategy

The itinerary is built knee-aware. Two days need a plan.

  • Fushimi-Inari (24th): stay at the base — the famous dense torii are there; skip the 2–3h summit
  • Mt Misen (28th): ropeway up + the rocky ~30-min summit trail. Trekking poles, proper shoes, go up early for a guaranteed ropeway-down buffer, turn back at the observatory if it protests
  • Everything else is flat/gentle by design

🌡️ Heat & weather

Late July = hot and humid (28–34°C), tail of the rainy season into peak summer. No snow anywhere you go.

  • Light breathable clothes, compact umbrella, cooling towel, sun protection, hand fan
  • Kyoto (basin) is the sweatiest stretch; Koyasan (~800m) is a cool relief — bring a layer for it
  • Hydrate relentlessly, especially on festival and shrine days

🎆 The two festivals

You landed on both by luck — they fall on your Kyoto days.

  • Gion Matsuri (24th): Ato float parade in Kyoto — ask your guide; treat as a bonus
  • Tenjin Matsuri (25th): locked — Kyoto→Osaka dash for the boats + 5,000 fireworks. Nail the exit (linger, then walk to Umeda)

🏨 Where you sleep

The tour covers 11 nights (20–31). You arrange the bookends.

  • 18–19 Jul: Tokyo — simplest via TruTravels at the joining hostel (pre-night: email info@trutravels.com)
  • 31 Jul: stay on at the TruTravels hostel (book the extra post-tour night with them) — no luggage move on checkout day
  • Insurance: compulsory for TruTravels (min US$200k medical/evac) — and needed for the visa

Japan visa — what you need ビザ

Indian passport holders need a short-stay tourist visa for Japan, applied for before travel. You're routing via Atlys. Apply early — summer processing runs ~3–6 weeks. The day-by-day itinerary and funds proof get the most scrutiny.

📄 Core documents

  • Passport — valid 6+ months beyond travel, with 2+ blank pages
  • Visa application form — filled, signed (Atlys provides/assists)
  • Photo — 45×45mm, white background, taken within 6 months (note: this is square, NOT Schengen size)
  • Flight booking — confirmed round-trip (your Cathay BOM→HND / KIX→BOM)
  • Accommodation — all hotel/hostel bookings covering the full stay
  • Cover letter — addressed to the Embassy/Consulate, stating purpose, dates, self-funding

💰 Financial proof

  • Bank statements — last 6 months, showing sufficient balance for the trip
  • ITR — income tax returns for the last 2–3 years
  • Salary slips — last 3–6 months
  • Sufficient balance: enough to credibly cover ~16 days (rule of thumb ~₹1.5–2L visible, but more comfort is better)

💼 Employment proof

  • Leave sanction letter from Tribeca — on company letterhead, confirming approved leave for the travel dates and your return
  • Employment certificate / NOC — stating designation, salary, length of service
  • Business card (helpful, not mandatory)

🗺 The itinerary (scrutinised)

  • Day-by-day itinerary — date / city / accommodation / activities, matching your flights & bookings exactly
  • This is the document the consulate reads most closely — it must be consistent and realistic
  • Travel insurance — covering the full trip (also required by TruTravels: min US$200k medical/evac)
  • I can build the cover letter + this itinerary doc for you — just ask
Process & timing

Japan tourist-visa applications for Indians go through VFS Global — and as of 2026 the Mumbai centre is appointment-based (walk-in submissions were discontinued and the consulate no longer takes direct applications). Book a VFS slot in advance; Atlys handles the legwork for you. Summer is peak — apply as early as possible (aim 4–6 weeks before departure). Typical processing is several working days once submitted, but appointment availability + courier time stretch it out. If you're issued an eVISA rather than a passport sticker, remember the Visa Issuance Notice must be displayed live online on your phone at the airport — no PDF, screenshot or printout. Keep digital + physical copies of everything, and make sure every date and booking reference lines up across the cover letter, the itinerary, and your flight/hotel confirmations — inconsistencies are the most common cause of delays. Confirm the latest submission and appointment rules on the official VFS Global India and Mumbai Consulate sites before applying.
