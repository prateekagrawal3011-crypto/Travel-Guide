Lucky timing

The festivals you landed on

Your dates line up with one of Japan's Three Great Festivals — and your finale is the country's biggest music event. Two cultural jackpots, baked into the trip.

🏮 Gion Matsuri · Kyoto

1,150 years old · all July · you catch the climax
›21–23 Jul — Ato Yoiyama evenings: downtown Kyoto closes to traffic, lantern-lit floats glow, food stalls line the streets, locals stroll in yukata (6–11pm). You're in Kyoto on the tour for this.
›24 Jul — Ato Matsuri float parade: the giant yamaboko floats roll through downtown (~9:30–11:50am). Lands on your solo Kyoto day — and it's the quieter, better-for-photos second parade.
›Eat: yakitori, takoyaki, taiyaki, kakigori (shaved ice) from the stalls. Wear something light; it's hot.

🎸 Fuji Rock · Naeba

Japan's biggest music festival · 24–26 Jul
›Your day: Sunday 26 Jul — a 1-day pass (Saturday's sold out; Sunday still available as of June). 200+ artists across forest stages in a mountain valley.
›2026 names include The xx, Khruangbin, Fujii Kaze, Basement Jaxx, Mitski, Massive Attack, Mogwai and many more across rock, electronic, indie & jazz.
›The vibe: stages strung along a crystal river through the forest — famous for its setting and communal spirit as much as the music.
🎆

Also on (if you'd reshaped differently): Osaka's Tenjin Matsuri (24–25 Jul, boat procession + fireworks) and Tokyo's Sumida River Fireworks (last Sat of July) — both spectacular, but they clash with the Fuji Rock plan. You chose the mountain; it's the right call for a music trip.

The finale

Fuji Rock, in detail

Despite the name it's nowhere near Mt Fuji — it's at Naeba Ski Resort in the Niigata mountains, ~2 hrs north of Tokyo, and it's a genuinely special way to end the trip. Here's how to do your Sunday cleanly.

🎟️

Ticket — book NOW

Get the 1-Day Sunday pass (¥27,500) before it sells out — Saturday already has. Buy via the official site / Ticket Pia / Eplus. You exchange it for a wristband at the gate.

🚄

Getting there

Jōetsu Shinkansen Tokyo → Echigo-Yuzawa (~90 min, ¥6,790), then the festival shuttle bus to Naeba (~40 min, ¥500 in / free back, runs 5am–2am). Enter the station as "Echigo-Yuzawa."

🛏️

Where to sleep — book NOW

Naeba/Echigo-Yuzawa lodges sell out fast. Base in Echigo-Yuzawa (on the Shinkansen, shuttle-linked) for Sat & Sun nights, or day-trip from Tokyo if lodging's gone (long but doable).

🦵

Knee-smart festival

The site is hilly forest paths between stages. Pace it: pick a couple of stages, bring a lightweight fold-stool, comfy broken-in shoes, and don't try to see everything. One day, your rhythm.

🌦️

Weather-proof

Mountain weather is famously unpredictable — sun and heavy rain both likely. Pack a poncho (not umbrella), quick-dry layers, and a dry bag for your phone. It can get muddy.

🍢

The food is great

Fuji Rock's food stalls are legendary — far beyond festival junk. Curry, ramen, craft beer, world street food along the river. Your Shred can survive one day; enjoy it.

⚡

Two things to lock this week: the 1-Day Sunday ticket and Echigo-Yuzawa accommodation for 25 & 26 July. Both sell out for Fuji Rock, and they're the only time-critical pieces of the whole trip. Everything else can wait.

Multi-share is the trade

4 tour nights are hostel multi-share (4–6/room, shared baths) — the known compromise for these dates + crowd. Japan's hostels are spotless; earplugs + eye mask sort it.

🎒

Pack carry-on only

Shinkansen luggage must be under 160cm total + a day pack. You carry your own bag through stations & up stairs — and to Fuji Rock. Travel light; a soft carry-on is ideal.

🧳

Luggage hack

Use Japan's takuhaibin service to forward your main bag (e.g. Kyoto → Tokyo hotel) so you do Fuji Rock with just a day pack. Cheap, reliable, overnight — most hotels arrange it.

Staying on protocol

The Shred, in Japan

Japan might be the best food culture for a cut — lean protein everywhere, controlled portions, low-oil cooking. Hold the line while still eating the good stuff (and surviving two festival days).

🐟

Sashimi & grilled fish

Sashimi, grilled salmon/mackerel, yakitori — high protein, low carb, minimal oil. The default at any sushi spot or izakaya.

🥚

Konbini is your friend

7-Eleven/Lawson: boiled eggs, salad chicken, edamame, Greek yogurt — macro-perfect grabs between sights and on travel days.

🚶

Steps = cardio

Tokyo & Kyoto are huge walking cities — 15–20k steps/day, flat and paved. Knee-friendly NEAT cardio without a gym.

🍜

The carb traps

Ramen, katsu, mochi, festival stalls — enjoy intentionally on the big days (nights out, Gion, Fuji Rock), pre-logged, not by default.

🍺

Night-out plan

Karaoke + Fuji Rock = drinks. Highballs & shochu-soda beat beer/sake on calories. Hydrate, treat-window them, tighten around.

🎪

Festival-day macros

Two festival days (Gion food stalls, Fuji Rock). Anchor each with a protein konbini breakfast, then enjoy the stalls. Two flexible days in twelve won't derail a thing.

What you'll eat

The table

Conveyor sushi to festival stalls — and plenty for vegetarians (handy for the veg eaters back home). The shortlist worth chasing.

Sushi & Sashimi

Conveyor (kaiten) sushi in Tokyo — cheap, fresh, fun. Tsukiji Outer Market for the real deal.

Ramen

Tokyo shoyu, rich tonkotsu — a once-or-twice treat. Ticket-machine shops are the move.

Hida Beef

Takayama's famous wagyu — grilled or as sushi on Sake Street. A regional splurge on your ryokan day.

Festival stalls

Gion & Fuji Rock: yakitori, takoyaki, taiyaki, kakigori, curry, craft beer. Street food at its best.

Vegetarian

Shojin Ryori

Kyoto Buddhist temple cuisine — elegant, fully plant-based. A veg highlight.

Vegetarian

Veg ramen & tofu

Vegetable/miso ramen, agedashi tofu, tempura, inari sushi — look for "yasai." Plenty meat-free.
